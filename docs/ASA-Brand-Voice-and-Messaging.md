# American Skin Art - Brand Voice & Messaging Guide

## Brand Identity

### Who We Are
American Skin Art is more than a tattoo studio—it's North Tonawanda's oldest tattoo shop with deep roots in the Buffalo/716 community. We're the intersection of tradition and evolution, maintaining the shop's historical legacy while building toward a modern, professional future.

### Core Values
1. **Quality over Speed** - Every tattoo is approached with care and precision
2. **Safety First** - Professional, sterile environment is non-negotiable
3. **Longevity Matters** - Designs that age well and stand the test of time
4. **Conversation Before Ink** - Every tattoo starts with understanding the client
5. **Community Connection** - Deep ties to Buffalo, the 716, Bills Mafia, and Western New York

### Mission Statement
To provide custom, high-quality tattoo work in a professional environment where every piece starts with a conversation, prioritizing safety, care, and long-lasting results while honoring our community's history and culture.

## Communication Guidelines

### Client Interactions

**First Contact (Consultation Request):**
- Acknowledge quickly
- Show enthusiasm for their idea
- Ask clarifying questions
- Set clear next steps
- Be professional but warm

**During Consultation:**
- Listen more than you talk
- Understand the "why" behind their design
- Be honest about what will work
- Provide timeline and pricing transparency
- Make them feel confident in their choice

**Appointment Reminder:**
- Confirm date, time, artist
- Reminder of what to bring
- Aftercare preview
- Express excitement to create with them

**Post-Appointment:**
- Aftercare instructions (written)
- Check-in after first week
- Invitation to share healed photos
- Thank them for trusting us

### Difficult Conversations

**Can't Do This Design:**
- Explain why honestly
- Offer alternatives
- Never shame or judge
- Help them find a solution

**Pricing Concerns:**
- Explain value, not just cost
- Break down time and expertise
- Emphasize longevity and quality
- Stand firm but be understanding

**No-Shows/Cancellations:**
- Clear policy, consistently applied
- Professional but firm
- Path to reschedule (if appropriate)
- Protect artists' time

## Crisis Communication

### Potential Issues & Responses

**Client Dissatisfaction:**
1. Listen and understand concern
2. Review work objectively
3. Offer touch-up if warranted
4. Document everything
5. Learn and improve

**Health/Safety Concerns:**
1. Address immediately and seriously
2. Refer to medical professional if needed
3. Review procedures internally
4. Document and follow up
5. Transparency with client

**Negative Review:**
1. Respond professionally, never defensively
2. Offer to discuss privately
3. If mistake made, own it
4. Show how you've improved
5. Let good work speak louder than one bad review

**Construction/Closure:**
1. Communicate timeline clearly
2. Multiple updates via email, social, website
3. Maintain appointment commitments
4. Celebrate the progress toward new space
